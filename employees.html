First 90 Days

W2 ONBOARDING TIMELINE

01

Pre-Hire (Before Day 1)

Submit all paperwork. Background check and drug screening completed. Review the employee handbook. Confirm your first day time and location with the office.

02

Day 1 — Orientation

Office orientation, meet the team, sign all remaining documents. Safety training walkthrough, PPE issuance. Introduction to your crew lead. Tour of an active job site.

03

Week 1–2 — Shadow & Learn

Work alongside experienced crew members. Learn material handling, staging, safety protocols, and quality standards. No solo work at this stage — observation and assisted tasks only.

04

Week 3–4 — Supervised Work

Begin performing tasks with crew lead oversight. Feedback provided daily. Questions encouraged — we'd rather answer them than have errors go unreported.

05

30-Day Check-In

Informal conversation with your manager. Review how things are going, address any concerns, confirm your role fit. This is a two-way conversation — your feedback matters.

06

90-Day Review

Formal performance review. Probationary period ends, PTO accrual begins, and you're officially full-status. Compensation adjustments and advancement discussions happen here.

SITE POLICIES & SAFETY.

Safety First, Always

Hard hat, safety glasses, gloves, and fall protection harness are required on every job site, every time. No exceptions. Safety violations result in immediate removal from site.

Zero Tolerance: Substances

No alcohol or drugs on any job site, period. If there's any suspicion of impairment, you will be sent home. Florida heat is serious — stay hydrated, stay sharp.

Customer Interaction

Be respectful and professional — always. Don't quote prices, make promises, or give assessments to homeowners. All customer communication goes through the project manager.

Communication Protocol

Text or call your PM if you'll be late, have a site issue, or need anything. We respond fast. Don't wait until a problem gets bigger — early communication saves everyone time and money.

Photos & Social Media

Sharing job site photos on social media requires PM approval. Never post homeowner property without consent. Great work photos are encouraged — just ask first.

Dress Code

Happy Roof shirt (or professional workwear), work pants/shorts, and steel-toe boots on every job site. No offensive graphics or logos. We represent the brand on every property.

Questions

NEW HIRE FAQ.

When do I get my first paycheck?

W2 employees are on a bi-weekly schedule — your first paycheck covers the first full pay period you work in. Typically 2–3 weeks after your start date. 1099 contractors are paid upon invoice approval (Net 7–14 days).

What happens if it rains or the job site is unsafe?

Your PM will communicate day-of changes as early as possible — usually by 6am if weather looks bad. Never work in unsafe conditions. If you arrive and something feels wrong, call your PM immediately. W2 employees may be assigned shop work or prep tasks on rain days.

Who do I call if I'm running late or have an emergency?

Text or call your project manager directly. Their contact is given during orientation. If you can't reach them, call the main office at (813) 595-7663. We don't penalize for communication — we penalize for no-shows without a heads-up.

As a 1099, can I work for other roofing companies?

Yes — you're an independent contractor, not an exclusive employee. However, while you're on an active Happy Roof job site or working a project for us, we expect your full attention and professional conduct. Non-compete clauses, if applicable, are outlined in your contractor agreement.

What if I see a safety issue on site?

Stop work and report it to your crew lead or PM immediately. There's no penalty for flagging safety concerns — there's a serious consequence for ignoring them. We take OSHA compliance seriously and encourage every team member to speak up.

How can I move up within Happy Roof?

We promote from within. Crew leads, field supervisors, and project managers have all come up from entry-level roles. Show up consistently, communicate well, and deliver quality work — those are the three things that get noticed. Bring it up in your 90-day review.
