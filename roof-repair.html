Leak Detection

PROFESSIONAL LEAK DETECTION & DIAGNOSIS

Not all roof leaks are obvious. Water can travel along rafters, trusses, and sheathing before emerging as a ceiling stain far from the actual entry point. That is why accurate leak detection requires experience, training, and a systematic approach — not guesswork.

Visual Roof Inspection

We start with a comprehensive visual inspection of your entire roof surface — checking for missing, cracked, or curling shingles, damaged flashing, compromised sealant, and signs of wear around penetrations like vents, pipes, and skylights. We also inspect your attic from the inside, looking for daylight penetration, moisture stains, and mold growth.

Water Testing

For leaks that are difficult to pinpoint visually, we use controlled water testing to isolate the entry point. By systematically wetting specific areas of the roof while monitoring the interior, we can identify exactly where water is penetrating — even when the stain on your ceiling is several feet from the actual source.

Detailed Repair Report

After diagnosis, you receive a detailed written report with photos documenting the damage, the identified leak source(s), and our recommended repair plan with pricing. If the damage is covered by insurance, this report serves as supporting documentation for your claim. No surprises, no guesswork.

Shingle Repair

SHINGLE REPLACEMENT & REPAIR

Missing, cracked, curling, or wind-lifted shingles leave your roof deck exposed to water, UV damage, and further deterioration. Even a few compromised shingles can lead to leaks, mold growth, and structural damage if left unaddressed. Happy Roof replaces damaged shingles with matching materials — restoring your roof's protection and appearance without the cost of a full replacement.

We carry a wide inventory of GAF, Owens Corning, and other major-brand shingles to ensure a close color and profile match with your existing roof. If your shingles are discontinued or heavily weathered, we will let you know upfront and discuss options — including partial re-roofing of the affected slope for a uniform appearance.

All shingle repairs include inspection of the underlying felt or synthetic underlayment and decking. If we find rot, delamination, or moisture damage beneath the shingles, we address it before installing new material — because patching over a bad substrate just delays the problem.

Flashing Repair

FLASHING REPAIR & REPLACEMENT

Roof flashing — the metal strips that seal joints, valleys, chimneys, vents, skylights, and wall-to-roof transitions — is one of the most critical components of your roofing system. When flashing corrodes, lifts, or separates, it creates a direct path for water into your home. In fact, damaged or improperly installed flashing is the number one cause of roof leaks in Tampa Bay.

Happy Roof repairs and replaces all types of roof flashing, including step flashing, counter flashing, valley flashing, drip edge, pipe boots, and chimney flashing. We use corrosion-resistant aluminum and galvanized steel — never cheap galvanized tin that rusts in Florida's humidity.

Every flashing repair we perform is sealed with high-quality roofing sealant and mechanically fastened to code. We do not use roof cement as a substitute for proper flashing installation — a common shortcut that leads to callbacks and repeat leaks.

Decision Guide

WHEN TO REPAIR VS. WHEN TO REPLACE

Not every roof problem requires a full replacement. But sometimes a repair is just a band-aid on a bigger issue. Here is how to tell the difference — and Happy Roof will always give you an honest recommendation.

REPAIR Makes Sense When...

  • + Damage is isolated to one area or a few shingles
  • + Your roof is less than 15 years old
  • + There is a single, identifiable leak point
  • + Flashing around one penetration has failed
  • + The decking underneath is still solid

REPLACE Makes Sense When...

  • + Your roof is 20+ years old with widespread wear
  • + Multiple areas are leaking or showing damage
  • + Decking is rotted or delaminated in multiple spots
  • + You have had repeated repairs in the last few years
  • + Insurance is covering storm damage to the full roof
