HOW LONG DOES A ROOF REPLACEMENT TAKE? COMPLETE TIMELINE

One of the most common questions Tampa Bay homeowners ask when facing a roof replacement is "How long is this going to take?" It is a fair question — a roof replacement affects your daily routine, your work schedule, your pets, and your peace of mind. The answer depends on several factors, but this guide gives you a realistic, phase-by-phase timeline so you know exactly what to expect from first phone call to final inspection.

THE SHORT ANSWER

For a typical Tampa Bay residential roof replacement:

  • Asphalt shingle roof: 1 to 3 days of actual installation
  • Metal roof (standing seam): 3 to 5 days of installation
  • Tile roof: 5 to 7 days of installation

But the installation days are only part of the story. The total process — from your initial call to the final inspection — typically takes 2 to 6 weeks depending on permit processing times, material availability, weather, and your contractor's schedule. Here is the full timeline broken down by phase.

PHASE 1: ESTIMATE AND CONTRACT (DAYS 1-7)

The process begins when you contact a roofing contractor for an estimate. A reputable contractor will schedule an on-site inspection of your existing roof — not just a drive-by look, but a detailed assessment that includes measuring the roof, inspecting the decking condition from the attic (if accessible), documenting any existing damage, and assessing ventilation.

What Happens During the Estimate Visit

  • Roof measurements (often supplemented by aerial/satellite measurements)
  • Assessment of existing roof condition and number of existing layers
  • Attic inspection for ventilation, insulation, and decking condition
  • Discussion of material options, colors, and budget
  • Identification of any structural concerns or code upgrade requirements

After the inspection, you will receive a detailed written estimate covering materials, labor, permits, and any additional work needed (decking replacement, ventilation upgrades, etc.). Take time to review it, ask questions, and compare with other estimates if desired. Once you sign the contract and provide the initial deposit, the project moves to the permitting phase.

If you need help with the financial side, ask about financing options — this is the time to get that sorted so it does not create delays later.

PHASE 2: PERMITTING (3-10 BUSINESS DAYS)

Every roof replacement in Tampa Bay requires a building permit from the local county building department. Your contractor handles this process entirely, but understanding the timeline helps set expectations.

  • Hillsborough County: Typically 1-3 business days for standard residential re-roofs
  • Pinellas County: Typically 1-5 business days
  • Pasco County: Typically 2-5 business days

During high-volume periods — such as after a major storm or during peak construction season — these timelines can stretch. Some counties offer expedited permitting for an additional fee. Your contractor files a Notice of Commencement with the county, submits product specifications and installation details, and receives the approved permit before any work begins.

While the permit is processing, your contractor orders materials and coordinates the delivery schedule. This is also when they slot your project into their installation calendar.

PHASE 3: MATERIAL DELIVERY (1-3 DAYS BEFORE INSTALLATION)

Roofing materials are typically delivered to your property 1 to 3 days before the scheduled installation date. For shingle roofs, the manufacturer or distributor delivers pallets of shingles, underlayment rolls, flashing materials, drip edge, and other supplies. For metal roofs, panels are often custom-cut to the exact measurements of your roof and shipped from the manufacturer.

Expect the delivery to take up some driveway or yard space temporarily. The crew will stage materials on the roof or near the work area on the morning of installation.

PHASE 4: INSTALLATION DAY(S)

This is the main event. Here is what happens on a typical shingle roof replacement, which is the most common type in Tampa Bay:

Day 1: Tear-Off and Deck Preparation

  1. Setup and protection (30-60 min) — The crew arrives early (usually 7:00-7:30 AM), sets up ladders and safety equipment, and lays tarps and protection around your home's perimeter to catch debris and protect landscaping, windows, and siding.
  2. Old roof removal (2-4 hours) — The existing roofing material, underlayment, and flashing are stripped down to the plywood or OSB decking. This is the noisiest part of the process.
  3. Deck inspection and repair (1-3 hours) — With the decking exposed, the crew inspects every square foot for rot, water damage, delamination, and structural issues. Damaged decking is replaced with new plywood or OSB. The extent of decking replacement varies — some roofs need none, while others may require significant sections to be replaced.
  4. Underlayment installation (1-2 hours) — A self-adhering modified bitumen underlayment (peel-and-stick) is applied to the roof deck as the secondary water barrier required by Florida Building Code. Drip edge is installed at eaves and rakes.

Day 1-2: Shingle Installation

  1. Starter course and field shingles (4-8 hours) — Starter strip shingles are installed along eaves and rakes, followed by the field shingles working from the bottom of the roof upward. Each shingle is fastened with a specific nail pattern to meet Florida's wind code requirements.
  2. Flashing installation (1-2 hours) — New flashing is installed around all penetrations (pipes, vents, skylights, chimneys) and at wall-to-roof transitions. This is one of the most critical steps for preventing future leaks.
  3. Ridge cap and ventilation (1-2 hours) — Hip and ridge cap shingles are installed, along with any ridge vent or other ventilation components.
  4. Cleanup (1-2 hours) — The crew removes all debris, runs a magnetic nail sweep around the property, and hauls away old materials in a dumpster that was placed before work began.

For metal roofing, add 1-3 additional days depending on the panel system and roof complexity. Metal panels require more precise installation, custom trim fabrication on-site, and careful seam engagement that takes longer than shingle installation. Tile roofs require the most time due to the weight of materials and the precision of the installation process.

PHASE 5: FINAL INSPECTION (1-5 BUSINESS DAYS AFTER INSTALLATION)

After installation is complete, your contractor schedules a final inspection with the county building department. A building inspector visits your property to verify that the roof was installed according to the permitted plans and meets all applicable Florida Building Code requirements.

The inspector checks material specifications, fastener patterns, flashing, drip edge, underlayment, and overall workmanship. If the roof passes, the permit is closed. If corrections are needed, the contractor addresses them and schedules a re-inspection.

FACTORS THAT AFFECT YOUR TIMELINE

Several variables can shorten or lengthen the overall process:

  • Weather delays — Roofing cannot be done in the rain. During Tampa Bay's summer rainy season (June-September), afternoon thunderstorms can halt work for the day. Your contractor should have contingency plans for weather delays.
  • Roof size and complexity — A simple gable roof on a 1,500 sq ft ranch home goes much faster than a complex multi-hip roof on a 3,000 sq ft two-story with multiple penetrations, skylights, and dormers.
  • Decking replacement — If significant decking needs to be replaced (more than 10-15 sheets), it adds time to the installation. The extent of decking damage is often not fully known until the old roof is removed.
  • Material availability — Specialty materials, unusual colors, or custom-cut metal panels may have longer lead times. Standard colors and products from GAF and Owens Corning are typically in stock at local distributors.
  • Contractor schedule — During peak season (post-hurricane, spring rush before hurricane season), wait times to get on a contractor's schedule can be longer. Booking during the off-season (October-February) often means faster scheduling.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

Yes, you can stay in your home during a roof replacement in most cases. The work is noisy — expect hammering, nail guns, and the sound of old materials being removed — so plan accordingly if you work from home or have small children. Dust and debris will be present around the exterior of the house. Your contractor should take precautions to protect your landscaping, vehicles, and outdoor furniture. If you have pets, keep them inside and away from exterior doors where crew members are moving in and out.
The ideal time for roof replacement in Tampa Bay is during the dry season — roughly October through May. This window offers lower humidity, fewer afternoon thunderstorms, and cooler working temperatures. However, roofs can be replaced year-round in Florida. Summer installations are common but may experience more weather delays due to daily thunderstorms. If you need a roof replacement before hurricane season (June 1), try to schedule it by March or April to avoid the spring rush.
Permit processing times vary by county. In Hillsborough County, residential roofing permits are often issued within 1-3 business days for straightforward re-roofs. Pinellas County typically processes within 1-5 business days. Pasco County is similar at 2-5 business days. However, these timelines can extend during high-volume periods (post-storm, peak construction season). Some jurisdictions offer expedited or same-day permitting for an additional fee. Your roofing contractor handles the permit process, so you do not need to visit the building department yourself.
